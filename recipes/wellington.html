Chef John's Beef Wellington

Already tried Gordon Ramsay's famous Beef Wellington recipe? If you've already attempted the notorious chef's interpretaion of this classic Christmas dish, you might want to turn your eyes toward the underrated Wellington recipe of one of the oldest cooking YouTube channels. Though not as glamorous as the British culinary sensation, chef John's humble, sing-songy approach to culinary arts and presentation might just leave you pleasntly surprised.

Ingredients: Mushroom Duxelles

  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 10 large brown mushrooms, finely chopped
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons minced shallots
  • freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 pinch cayenne pepper, or to taste
  • 3 tablespoons white wine
  • 2 ounces pate

Ingredients: For the Wellington

  • 4 (8 ounce) filet mignon steaks
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons salted butter
  • 1 (17.25 ounce) package frozen puff pastry
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 1 teaspoon water

Directions

  1. Cook mushrooms, shallots, salt, and spices in butter until browned and caramelized. Let cool.
  2. Mix mushroom mixture with pate until well combined. Set aside.
  3. Add pate to cooled mushroom mixture; toss and mash with a spoon until thoroughly combined. Set aside.
  4. Season filets with salt and pepper. Sear in butter until browned. Transfer to a plate and refrigerate.
  5. Roll out puff pastry and cut into squares. Place a portion of the mushroom mixture on each square.
  6. Spread mustard on one side of each filet. Place mustard-side down on the mushroom mixture.
  7. Fold the pastry around the filet to seal, leaving the ends open. Place on a baking sheet.
  8. Bake in a preheated oven at 425°F (220°C) for 15 minutes. Flip and bake for an additional 5-10 minutes until cooked through and crispy.
  9. Transfer the Wellington to the freezer for 15 minutes.
  10. Prepare a baking sheet with aluminum foil and parchment paper. Beat an egg with water and brush the Wellingtons with the egg wash.
  11. Bake in the preheated oven until the pastry is golden brown and puffed, and the filets are cooked through, about 23-25 minutes.
  12. Remove from the oven and let rest for 5 minutes. Cut the Wellingtons in half and trim the ends.
  13. Place each pastry base on a plate and top with two filet halves, cut side up. Serve immediately.
